# Etch — Brand Guide

## Brand Identity

**Etch** — To carve into a surface. Something permanent, physical, tactile. Not cloud, not sync, not subscription. You etch your feelings into something real. The name implies permanence without the weight of "archive" or "legacy."

### Brand Promise
*"The place the algorithm can't see."*

### Brand Personality
- **Not** corporate, enterprise, or "SaaS"
- **Not** warm, friendly, "helpful assistant," or "wellness coach"
- **Not** millennial, nostalgic, or archival
- **Is:** quiet, private, warm in a dark room, a little mysterious
- **Vibe:** If Apple Journal is a corporate office, Etch is a candlelit room at 2am

### Tone of Voice
- Quiet. No exclamation marks. No "Hey there! 👋"
- Honest without being harsh
- Anti-corporate without being edgy about it
- Short sentences. Fragments. Like a person thinking, not a brand marketing.
- "Your thoughts are yours. We can't see them. Nobody can." — this is the voice

### Target Audience

**Gen Z (15-25).** They journal to decompress, not to archive. They're anxious, overstimulated, surveilled. They want one space that's theirs. They found #Journaling on TikTok (4.5B views) and #ShadowWork (2B views). They don't want a "tool" — they want a sanctuary.

Anyone else who buys the app is welcome, but the product is not designed for them.

### Visual Direction

**Not:** Glassmorphism, gradients, emoji, cute illustrations, corporate wellness, bright colors, "consumer app" look, HR portal vibes
**Is:** OLED black, deep atmosphere, tactile textures, warm accents in darkness, film grain, physical feeling on a digital surface

**References:** Dark mode Instagram (but warmer), the feeling of a physical journal in a dark room, Lo-fi aesthetics, the "romanticize your life" TikTok vibe

### App Icon Direction
- Minimal. 1-2 colors max.
- Not: letter "E", notebook icon, pencil icon, quill
- Consider: abstract mark, organic shape, something that feels carved or etched
- Must work at 60x60 (widget) and 1024x1024 (App Store)
- No gradient. No skeuomorphism. Flat or subtle depth.
- Vibe: warm mark on black background. A glow in the dark.

### Film Grain

Subtle film grain texture overlaid on the canvas background. Barely perceptible — you feel it more than you see it. This breaks the "glass screen" feeling that Gen Z is exhausted by. The app should feel like a surface, not a screen.

Technical: 2-3% opacity noise texture, animated very slowly (almost imperceptible shift over 30 seconds). Or static texture image at low opacity.

### Marketing Voice

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "The algorithm can't see this." | "Privacy-first architecture" |
| "Your thoughts don't have a monthly rent." | "Affordable one-time pricing" |
| "No server. No tracking. Nobody watching." | "Data persistence guaranteed" |
| "For 2am, not for your resume." | "Designed for mindful reflection" |
| "It's just you and your mind." | "Comprehensive journaling solution" |

### What Etch Is NOT

- NOT a productivity tool
- NOT a "second brain"
- NOT a gratitude journal
- NOT a mood tracking app (it has mood features, but mood is expression, not measurement)
- NOT a social platform
- NOT a therapy replacement
- NOT a millennial archive
- NOT a note-taking app

### Naming

Just **Etch.** No colon. No subtitle. No "Etch: Journal." The app is one thing. It doesn't need a category label.

### App Store Presence

- **Name:** Etch
- **Subtitle:** A private journal
- **Category:** Health & Fitness (not Productivity — this is mental health, not output)
- **Keywords:** journal, diary, private, mental health, shadow work, mood, anxiety, journaling, no subscription, offline journal
